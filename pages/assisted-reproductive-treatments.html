What do we offer?

Fertility Evaluation- you might have done it already, but we recommend a confirmatory evaluation and diagnosis by our partner specialists, all of whom hold QHA Trent or equivalent accreditation. This would normally involve both prospective parents, so that the causes of any fertility disorder are fully identified and to ensure you receive the most appropriate treatment, with the maximum chance of success.

Fertility Treatments- our partners have had great success in less invasive treatments including:

  • Ovulation induction and stimulation;
  • Intrauterine insemination - using partner's or donor's sperm;
  • Superovulation - to increase the quantity and quality of egg production.

In-vitro fertilization (IVF)- our partners are able to conduct the full range and most appropriate form of IVF treatments, where this is considered the best solution. Treatments can include the following:

  • Natural cycle - where the mother's naturally occurring egg is surgically removed, fertilized and implanted.
  • Hormone stimulation - the mother is treated to produce the optimum number and quality of eggs which are extracted, fertilized and the most viable egg or eggs are implanted.
  • Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI) - where there may be an issue with the father's fertility, sperm is collected to be injected into the mother's egg.
  • TESE - in the most severe male infertility cases a biopsy extracts sperm from testicular tissue allowing the mother's egg to be fertilized using ICSI.

Egg Freezing (Cryocycle)- this is the freezing of fertilized, viable eggs, produced by hormone stimulation, for future use. This may be necessary for a number of reasons:

  • as a fall back in case of a failed IVF attempt;
  • for parents who need IVF but are hoping for subsequent children;
  • the presence of conditions that may affect the further reproductive ability of the parents.

Donors- where the reproductive health of either or both parents is in doubt, IVF using donor eggs and/or sperm may be appropriate. In such an event the following is considered:

  • donor eggs may be fertilized by the partner, where possible, or by a sperm donor, where it is not;
  • donor sperm may be used to fertilize the mother's eggs;
  • both parents must consent, in writing, to a donor procedure;
  • donors go through a rigorous medical, psychological, and genetic screening process;
  • donors are selected that closely match the blood type and phenotype (physical characteristics) of the parents.;

Surrogacy- the procedure by which the pregnancy is carried by a surrogate and not the mother. There are two main types of surrogacy programme:

  • both the egg/s and sperm of the future parents are used, which results in a baby that is 100% genetically linked to them;
  • donor cells (male and/or female) are used - in this case, the baby will not be genetically linked to the parents. However, careful selection of donors will take the parents' phenotype into consideration .
